Sample formats

Three formats. Three different jobs.

This portfolio is intentionally focused on the three outputs that best represent how I work: field-level synthesis, methodological appraisal, and deep single-paper evidence extraction.

Field view

Scientific Landscape Analysis

Broad synthesis of a literature set, built to surface major patterns, evidence gaps, and high-level scientific signals across multiple papers.

  • Best for understanding a field fast
  • Structured comparison and gap mapping
  • Useful for review framing and positioning

Comparative Appraisal Summary

Structured comparison of studies or claims, with attention to methodological quality, interpretive strength, and confidence boundaries.

  • Useful before building bigger arguments

Deep extraction

Structured Evidence Dossier

Deep-dive structured analysis of a single paper or focused scientific question, using an expanded evidence-extraction approach.

What this work is for

Not more papers. Better use of the papers you already have.

01

Understand the field

Clarify what is known, what remains uncertain, and where the real scientific signal sits across a literature set.

02

Judge reliability

Separate stronger evidence from weaker design, narrow claims, or overextended interpretation.

03

Structure one paper deeply

Turn dense articles into reusable, traceable evidence maps for review writing, thesis work, or research planning.

Who this is for

Built for readers who need rigor, not generic summaries.

MSc students

Useful when the challenge is making sense of a literature-heavy topic fast, without losing the scientific structure.

PhD researchers

Useful for review drafting, evidence mapping, methodological comparison, and clearer mechanistic interpretation.

Early-stage biotech and science-facing teams

Useful when literature needs to be turned into something structured, legible, and decision-relevant.

Process

From source material to structured scientific output.

01

Define the question

Clarify the scientific problem, evidence need, and unit of analysis.

02

Extract the evidence

Capture study design, models, endpoints, findings, and limitations without inflating the source.

03

Compare and interpret

Map convergence, contradiction, methodological strength, and confidence boundaries.

04

Deliver the output

Present a clear, traceable format that supports review work, analysis, or scientific positioning.

Executive Snapshot
Model context

In vitro TIVAP and TPN models plus in vivo rat TIVAP infections with E. coli LF82, 55989, and 55989pAT881 catheter biofilms.

Intervention vs comparator

High-dose amikacin lock therapy (continuous 7–10 days or intermittent cycles at 500× MIC) versus PBS lock and ancestral populations.

Principal outcome

Ten-day continuous ALTAMK eradicated biofilm bacteria without detectable amikacin resistance, while intermittent ALTAMK failed to eradicate biofilms and was linked to low-level resistance and elevated growth on amikacin plates.

Mechanistic signal

Convergent mutations in fusA, sbmA, and cpxA — plus additional genes including nuo and MFS transporters — emerged across intermittent lock conditions and are associated with aminoglycoside resistance, tolerance, and persistence.

Top-line takeaway

Intermittent high-dose amikacin exposure in structured catheter biofilms did not achieve eradication and coincided with low-level resistance. Extended continuous exposure eradicated biofilms without selecting detectable amikacin resistance in the models tested.

Key extracted finding
Central finding
Ten-day continuous amikacin lock therapy eradicated E. coli biofilms without favoring emergence of resistance, whereas intermittent amikacin lock therapy failed to fully eradicate biofilms and promoted the emergence of low-level amikacin resistance across multiple experimental settings.

5. Experimental Design and Evidence Structure

Models and Systems

Experimental models span in vitro and in vivo catheter-associated settings to capture resistance trajectories under different exposure schedules.

  • In vitro TIVAP model using pediatric 5 Fr TIVAPs with continuous LB flow.
  • In vitro TIVAP model using TPN in place of LB.
  • In vivo preclinical rat model with implanted TIVAPs reproducing clinically relevant catheter biofilm infection settings.
  • Bioluminescence imaging via IVIS Spectrum CT.
  • Whole-population genome sequencing of endpoint biofilm populations.

Replication and Analytical Parameters

Replicate numbers and analytical thresholds are maintained to enable assessment of robustness and detection limits.

  • In vitro groups: n = 3 biological replicates (TIVAPs) per group.
  • In vivo groups: n = 3 rats per group.
  • Internal volume of TIVAP ≈ 250 µL.
  • Continuous flow rate = 300 µL/min.
  • WGS average sequencing depth = 150×.
  • BreSeq detection limit = 5% mutation frequency.
  • LOD for CFU counting = 10 CFU/mL.
Context markers
For each result line in this dossier, context descriptors — model (in vitro LB, in vitro TPN, in vivo), strain, compartment (Si/Po/Ca), and phase (growth, lock, recovery) — are preserved exactly as extracted and are not merged across conditions.

10. Limitations, Quality, and Uncertainty

Reported limitations

Limitations are presented as extracted to support critical appraisal and appropriate use of this single-study evidence.

  • Small in vivo sample size (n = 3 per group) may limit statistical power.
  • Nature of tested strains and nutritional conditions may not fully mimic clinical settings.
  • Mechanisms behind rapid and higher-level in vivo resistance remain unclear.
  • Further in vivo work is needed to clarify dynamics; a continuous drip infusion pig catheter model was suggested.
  • In vivo TPN was administered as instillation, not continuous flow, because rats could not be immobilized.
  • Systemic ceftriaxone treatment in vivo may have influenced amikacin resistance dynamics.
